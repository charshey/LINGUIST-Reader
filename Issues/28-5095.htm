Job Rank: Full Professor

Specialty Areas: Translation; Interpreting

Required Language(s): English (eng)
                            French (fra)

                            Italian (ita)

                            Spanish (spa)

Description:

The University of Innsbruck invites applications for the position of University Professor of Translation Studies with a focus on Interpreting at the Department of Translation Studies of the Faculty of Humanities 2 (Language and Literature).

The position will be based on a permanent civil-law employment contract with the University.

Responsibilities:
- Representing the subject of “Translation Studies” both in research and teaching.
- A main research focus in the area of interpreting studies, a further research focus in translation theory.
- Collaboration with the other research and teaching areas in the department and across the university is expected. For the latter, the research centres “Cultures in Contact”, “Dimensions of Transfer” and “Digital Humanities” and the university-wide Research Area “Cultural Encounters – Cultural Conflicts” are particularly relevant.
- Teaching involves courses as foreseen in the BA and MA curriculums, supervision of MA dissertations and examination in the entire domain of Translation Studies.
- Participation in the academic self-administration is expected.

Eligibility requirements:
- a pertinent degree in higher education;
- a pertinent habilitation of equivalent qualification;
- relevant publications;
- competence in interpreting studies and translation theory;
- excellent command of German and full professional competence in at least one other language that is taught at the department (English, French, Italian, Spanish, Russian);
- involvement in international research;
- excellent didactic skills;
- experience in the procurement of research funding;
- social competence and ability to act as a leader;

Further desirable qualifications:
- competence in another language taught at the department;
- experience in interdisciplinary collaboration;
- relevant professional experience outside the university;
- relevant professional experience abroad.

The University of Innsbruck is committed to increasing the percentage of female employees, especially in leading positions and therefore explicitly invites women to apply. In the case of equivalent qualifications, women will be given preference.

Applications must reach us at the latest by 10 January 2018 and should include the usual documentation (esp. curriculum vitae, publications list, description of current and intended research, samples of the 5 most important works). All documents have to be submitted in digital form. Hardcopy (paper) is optional.

Please send the documents to the application address / email address below.

The basic salary is set down in the collective bargaining agreement for university employees. Professors are in the remuneration group A 1. For the position the monthly salary is 4.891,10 Euro (14 x). Depending on qualification and experience a higher salary can be a topic in the negotiations with the rector. Beyond that the university offers attractive additional benefits (http://www.uibk.ac.at/universitaet/zusatzleistungen/).
